The Addax roams the Sahara desert, and is well adapted to the extreme climate.

They are able to survive without free water for long periods of time, due to their ability to obtain water from their diet. They once lived across North Africa and the Middle East, and were even domesticated by the Ancient Egyptians.

Now, the Addax is critically endangered.

Addax are commonly hunted for their meat and leather. Human settlement, agriculture and oil exploration is destroying their habitat and making it harder to survive. It is estimated by the IUNC that less than 100 are left in the wild.

You can help protect the Addax.

Conservation work is ongoing, with many organisations working to maintain a captive Addax population with the goal of reintroducing them in the wild. To help, you can donate to the Sahara Conservation Fund or to African Wildlife Foundation.
