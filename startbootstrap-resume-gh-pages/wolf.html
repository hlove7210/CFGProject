The Ethiopian Wolf is a social animal, living in family packs of up to 20 individuals.

They are native to the highlands of Ethiopa, and are similar in build to the North American coyote.

They are now Africa's most endangered carnivore.

These wolves are vulnerable to diseases spread by wild and domesticated dogs, such as rabies. Human settlement and agriculture has also decreased their habitat. Less than 450 wolves are left in the wild.

We can save the Ethiopian Wolf.

Conservation efforts are underway, including vaccination programmes and habitat protection. To help, you can donate to the Ethiopian Wolf Conservation Programme or to the African Wildlife Foundation.
