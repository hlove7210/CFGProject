Rothschild's giraffes inhabit savannahs, grasslands, and open woodlands across Africa.

They are subspecies of the giraffe's family and are protected in Kenya and northern Uganda. They are related to deer and okapis. History indicates that these giraffes were once shaped more like deer. They grew their long necks out of necessity, and as a result, the rest of their body had to grow as well. Giraffes and okapis split from a common ancestor around 11.5 million of years ago.

To learn more - Read here!

Rothschild's giraffes have lost 40% of their population in just 30 years. Reports show that poaching and wildlife trafficking have contributed to this decline. According to the IUCN list, the four main threats associated with these species are habitat loss, civil unrest, illegal hunting and ecological changes.
