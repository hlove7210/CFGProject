Cheetahs are the world’s fastest land animals and are capable of sprinting at speeds up to 70 miles per hour.

They inhabit a variety of mostly arid habitats like dry forests, scrub forests, and savannahs.

Cheetahs are threatened by hunting, habitat loss and the decline of prey populations.

Just 7,100 of the big cats remain in the wild, spurring conservationists to call for the species to be declared endangered.
